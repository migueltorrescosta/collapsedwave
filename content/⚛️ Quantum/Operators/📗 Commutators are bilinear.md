# Proof
$$
\begin{array}{rll}
[xA + yB, C] & = (xA+yB)C - C(xA+yB) & \text{Expanding out the multiplications} \\
& = xAC + yBC - xCA - yCB & \text{Rearranging the algebraic expression} \\
& = x(AC-CA) + y(BC-CB) & \text{Applying the definition of Commutator} \\
& = x[A,C] + y[B,C]
\end{array}
$$
